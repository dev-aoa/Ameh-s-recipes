Crepe Recipe

Description

A crepe is a very thin pancake that originated in France. It is made from a mixture of flour, eggs, milk, and butter. The batter is poured onto a hot, flat griddle or pan and cooked until it is thin and delicate. Crepes are usually served with a variety of sweet or savory fillings, such as fruit, Nutella, cheese, ham, or vegetables. They can be folded or rolled into a tube shape and are often eaten as a dessert or breakfast dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour and eggs.
  2. Gradually add in the milk and water, stirring until the batter is smooth.
  3. Add in the salt and melted butter, and stir until well combined.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat. Scoop 1/4 cup of batter onto the pan and swirl it around to form a thin layer.
  5. Cook the crepe for 1-2 minutes on each side, or until lightly golden brown.
  6. Repeat with the remaining batter.
  7. Serve the crepes warm with your choice of toppings, such as fresh fruit, whipped cream, Nutella, or jam.
